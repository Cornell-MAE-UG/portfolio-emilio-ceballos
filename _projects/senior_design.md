AeroDry by ApolloTech was the project that satisfied my Senior Design requirement. This project was carried out through the course **MAE 4341: Innovative Product Design via Digital Manufacturing**, with the goal of experiencing every stage of the development of a new product. From market research, to ideation, to prototyping, and, finally, production, the team got to experience every part of the process.

The main purpose of this project was to create a device that could impact the day-to-day life of college students. Through extensive research and customer discovery, we found that one of the main, consistent issues that students struggled with involved laundry, particularly dryers. Many students commented on the dysfunctionality of on and off-campus dryers, noting that they often found themselves running 2 or even 3 cycles at a time to fully dry their clothes. Based on this feedback, the team designed an alternative to the conventional dryer, aiming to dry clothes quickly, effortlessly, and conveniently. The desired functions of this device were ease of installation, effective airflow, one-size-fits-all functionality, and durable parts. 

My role in this project involved carrying out CFD simulations to verify our assumptions regarding airflow, development of every iteration of our prototypes, customer discovery interviews, and developing our Choice-Based Conjoint Analysis survey.
